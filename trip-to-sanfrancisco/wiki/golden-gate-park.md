# Golden Gate Park

**Summary**: Massive urban park larger than Central Park, free to enter with paid museums inside. Best free half-day activity in San Francisco.

**Sources**: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-13

---

## Overview

Golden Gate Park is larger than Central Park and "significantly more interesting" according to the source. The park itself is free; museums inside charge admission. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Key Attractions Inside the Park

### de Young Museum
- **Cost**: $25 (free first Tuesday of month)
- **Hours**: Tue-Sun 9:30am-5:15pm
- **Free hack**: The observation tower has 360-degree views and is accessible for free without museum entry. Take it even if you skip the museum. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Japanese Tea Garden
- **Cost**: $12
- **Free hours**: Mon/Wed/Fri 9-10am
- **Significance**: Oldest public Japanese garden in the US
- **Website**: japanesetagarden.com

(source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Conservatory of Flowers
- **Cost**: $12
- **Description**: Victorian greenhouse worth 45 minutes

(source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Best Free Activity

Rent a bike ($15-40/day from nearby shops) and ride the JFK Promenade, which is closed to cars on weekends. The Sunday disco rollerskate gathering on JFK is "a San Francisco original" - free to watch, rentals available nearby. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Practical Details

- **Location**: Between Stanyan St and Ocean Beach
- **Hours**: Park always open
- **Cost**: Free (park); attractions inside have separate admission
