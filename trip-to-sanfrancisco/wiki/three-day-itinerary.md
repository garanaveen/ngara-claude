# Three-Day Itinerary

**Summary**: Optimal first visit to San Francisco covering essentials without rushing. Organized by geography to minimize travel time.

**Sources**: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-13

---

## Overview

"Three days is the right amount of time for a first visit to San Francisco." This itinerary is organized by geography to minimize travel time - "a common mistake with SF is zigzagging across the city and spending 30% of the day on Muni." (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Day 1: The Waterfront, North Beach & Chinatown

### Morning (8-10am)
Take BART to Embarcadero. Walk through the Ferry Building Marketplace - excellent coffee, artisan food stalls, farmers market (Tue/Thu/Sat). Buy breakfast here ($8-12). (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### 10am-1pm
Walk the Embarcadero north to Pier 33 for your [[alcatraz-island]] ferry (pre-booked). Allow 3 hours total including ferry. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### 2-3pm
Walk to [[north-beach]]. Browse City Lights bookstore. Beer at Vesuvio Café ($8-10). (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### 3-4pm
Walk Dragon's Gate into Chinatown - 15 minutes of tourist kitsch but atmospheric. Better option: walk to Clement Street in the Richmond for actual Chinese food ($10-15 for dim sum). (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Evening
Dinner in [[north-beach]] - Sotto Mare for cioppino ($26) or a walk-in Italian restaurant on Columbus Ave ($20-30/person). (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Day 2: Golden Gate, Presidio & Golden Gate Park

### Morning (9-10am)
Rent a bike at Bay City Bike near Fisherman's Wharf ($40/day). Ride through the Marina and Crissy Field to the [[golden-gate-bridge]]. Walk or ride across. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### 11am-12pm
Continue to Sausalito (30 min downhill). Lunch with bay views ($20-25). (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### 1-2pm
Return by ferry to Pier 41 ($16). Lock up bike or return to rental shop near Pier 39. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### 2-5pm
Walk/take Muni to [[golden-gate-park]]. Visit the Japanese Tea Garden or de Young Museum observation tower (free). If sunny, bring a blanket and join crowds on the main lawn. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Evening
Head to [[haight-ashbury]] for dinner and a drink at Magnolia Brewing ($15-20/person) or into the [[mission-district]] for burritos ($10-12). (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Day 3: Mission, Castro & Twin Peaks

### Morning
Coffee and pastry at Tartine Bakery (Mission) or Sightglass Coffee. Stroll Valencia Street - bookstores, vintage shops, local businesses. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Late Morning
Walk Clarion Alley (mural-lined alley, parallel to 17th St between Valencia & Mission). Worth 20 minutes. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### 12-2pm
Burrito at El Farolito or La Taqueria ($10-12). Sit in Dolores Park if weather is good. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### 2-4pm
Walk through the Castro neighborhood. Stop at Harvey Milk Plaza, browse shops on Castro Street. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### 4-5pm
Take Muni to Twin Peaks - 360-degree views of the entire city. Best at sunset (around 7:30pm in summer). Free, always accessible. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Evening
Dinner in the Castro or take BART back to your hotel area. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Trip Duration Notes

- **3 days**: "The sweet spot for a first visit" - enough time for [[alcatraz-island]], [[golden-gate-bridge]], neighborhoods, and good meals without rushing
- **5 days**: Allows for a day trip (Muir Woods, Sausalito, or Napa) and deeper neighborhood exploration
- **2 days**: Possible if you skip [[alcatraz-island]] and focus on free/fast experiences

(source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)
