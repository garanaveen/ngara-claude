# Haight-Ashbury

**Summary**: Historic 1960s counterculture neighborhood, still alive with record stores, thrift shops, and Victorian painted houses. More than just nostalgia.

**Sources**: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-13

---

## Overview

The Haight is "more than a nostalgia trip" - while you can visit historic sites like the Grateful Dead house (710 Ashbury Street, now private, exterior only) and Janis Joplin's former apartment, the neighborhood is still active and worth exploring. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Key Destinations

### Amoeba Records
"A genuine record store institution" with overwhelming selection. Free to browse. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Thrift Stores
The thrift stores are "legitimately good" according to the source. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Magnolia Brewing
Excellent beer and burgers. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Architecture

The painted Victorians here represent the classic "San Francisco house" image. The houses showcase the colorful Victorian style the city is known for. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Alamo Square Park
Short walk from Haight-Ashbury. Features the famous "Painted Ladies" row of Victorian houses with downtown skyline behind them - one of the most photographed scenes in San Francisco. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Getting There

N-Judah streetcar to Duboce Park, or walk ~20 minutes from Civic Center BART. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)
