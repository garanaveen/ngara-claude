# Food and Dining

**Summary**: San Francisco has one of the best food scenes in the US. The $4 burrito from Mission and $8 char siu bao from Chinatown will be more memorable than most Michelin restaurants. Skip Fisherman's Wharf food entirely.

**Sources**: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md

**Last updated**: 2026-04-13

---

## Overview

San Francisco has 55 Michelin-starred restaurants (2026), but the guide emphasizes that cheap authentic food is better than tourist infrastructure. "Don't waste meals on Fisherman's Wharf - that's tourist infrastructure, not real SF food." (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## What to Eat and Where

### Mission Burrito (Must-Do)
The guide calls these "the best burritos in the world (not an exaggeration)":
- **El Farolito** (24th & Mission)
- **La Taqueria** (Mission & 25th)
- **Taqueria Cancun**

All under $12, all excellent. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

See [[mission-district]] for full details.

### Dim Sum
- **Location**: Clement Street (Richmond District), NOT Chinatown
- **Budget**: $15-20/person
- **Splurge option**: Koi Palace

(source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Sourdough Bread
- **Tartine Bakery** (Mission)
- **Timing**: Arrive at 5pm when fresh loaves come out - expect a line

(source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Cioppino (SF Seafood Stew)
- **Sotto Mare** in [[north-beach]]
- **Price**: $26 for a full bowl
- **Note**: This is the real deal, unlike Fisherman's Wharf options

(source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Ramen
- **Marufuku** in [[japantown]]
- **Price**: $18
- **Wait**: Usually a queue, worth 30-45 minutes
- **Style**: Hakata-style tonkotsu

(source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Dungeness Crab (In Season: Nov-May)
- **Swan Oyster Depot** on Polk St
- **Price**: $35/half crab
- **Important**: Cash only, arrive early

(source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

### Coffee
- **Ritual Coffee Roasters** (multiple locations)
- **Blue Bottle** (started in Oakland)

(source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Food by Neighborhood

San Francisco food is shaped by Mexican, Chinese, Japanese, and Californian influences. The best food is in neighborhoods, not tourist areas:

- [[mission-district]]: Mexican, burritos, natural wine bars
- [[north-beach]]: Italian, cioppino, traditional restaurants
- [[japantown]]: Ramen, Japanese snacks, authentic dining
- Richmond District (Clement Street): Chinese dim sum, Asian food
- Polk Street: Swan Oyster Depot (seasonal crab)

## What to Skip

**Fisherman's Wharf food**: "Overpriced, mediocre." Example: $15+ for mediocre clam chowder. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)

## Budget Strategy

Eat like a local for cheap, excellent food:
- $3 Mission burrito
- $4 coffee
- $10-15 dim sum
- $12 pho
- $2-3 agua fresca from taqueria windows

This keeps daily food costs to $20-30 if you're on a budget. (source: Things to Do in San Francisco_ 2026 Guide to Neighborhoods, Costs & Insider Tips.md)
